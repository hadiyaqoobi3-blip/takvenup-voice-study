The Problem
No Modern ASR System Understands Dari
Current speech recognition treats Dari as Iranian Farsi. They are not the same. Dari has distinct phonology, vocabulary, and dialectal variation across Kabuli, Herati, Hazaragi, Panjshiri, and Mazari speakers.
1

No Open Dari Corpus

Dari (ISO 639-3: prs) has no entry in Common Voice, FLEURS, or ML-SUPERB. The only Dari speech data is behind military paywalls (IARPA Babel, 2012).

2

Zero Peer-Reviewed Papers

No published research fine-tunes modern transformer-based ASR (Whisper, wav2vec2, HuBERT) specifically on Dari as distinct from Iranian Farsi.

3

Dialect Variation Ignored

Hazaragi speakers have distinct vowel systems. Herati Dari is closer to Iranian Farsi. No ASR benchmark evaluates cross-dialect performance for Dari.

4

25M Speakers Underserved

Dari is the lingua franca of Afghanistan and spoken by large diaspora communities in the US, Europe, and Pakistan.

Research Methodology
This corpus follows established protocols from speech data collection research, community-based participatory methods (CBNLP), and UNHCR ethical guidelines for research with displaced populations.

Three Recording Tasks Per Speaker

Each participant completes three distinct tasks to capture controlled vocabulary, guided speech, and fully spontaneous patterns.

Task A

Scripted Read Speech (2 min)

Purpose: Controlled vocabulary with exact ground-truth transcription. Enables precise phoneme-level evaluation.

Read 8 short sentences covering high-frequency Dari phonemes and work vocabulary.

Task B

Guided Narrative (3 min)

Purpose: Spontaneous connected speech with predictable content. Captures natural disfluencies and dialectal features.

Respond to 3 open prompts about your work experience in your own words.

Task C

Free Narrative (3-5 min)

Purpose: Unrestricted spontaneous speech for domain-specific vocabulary and natural conversational style.

Tell us about a specific work experience in detail. A job-specific prompt guides you, but speak freely.
